Tianhe Ren

I am currently working at The International Digital Economy Academy (IDEA) as Computer Vision Engineer, advised by Prof. Lei Zhang . In 2021, I got my bachelor's degree from MAC Lab in Xiamen University advised by associate professor Yiyi Zhou and Prof. Rongrong Ji.

I am interested in computer vision and deep learning. My current research focuses on:

  • Instance-level recognition tasks including Object Detection, Instance Segmentation, Panoptic Segmentation.
  • General visual architectures and Multi-Modality tasks including the general visual backbone design, vision-language pretraining visual grounding, etc.
  • Deep generative models including GAN, Diffusion, etc.

    Detection Transformer with Stable Matching
    Shilong Liu*, Tianhe Ren*, Jiayu Chen*, Zhaoyang Zeng, Hao Zhang, Feng Li, Hongyang Li, Jun Huang, Hang Su, Jun Zhu, Lei Zhang
    arXiv preprint arXiv:2304.04742
    [arXiv] [Code]

    We propose two simple yet effective modifications by integrating positional metrics to DETR’s classification loss and matching cost, named positionsupervised loss and position-modulated cost to solve the unstable matching problem. And our Stable-DINO achieves 63.8AP on COCO test-dev with a Swin-Large backbone.

    Grounding DINO: Marrying DINO with Grounded Pre-Training for Open-Set Object Detection
    Shilong Liu, Zhaoyang Zeng, Tianhe Ren, Feng Li, Hao Zhang, Jie Yang, Chunyuan Li, Hang Su, Jun Zhu, Lei Zhang
    arXiv preprint arXiv:2303.05499
    [arXiv] [Code]

    GroundingDINO is an open-set object detector which can detect arbitrary objects with human inputs. GroundingDINO set a new record on COCO, LVIS, ODinW and RefCOCO/+/g.

    You Only Segment Once: Towards Real-Time Panoptic Segmentation
    Jie Hu, Linyan Huang, Tianhe Ren, Shengchuan Zhang, Rongrong Ji , Liujuan Cao ,
    IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR) , 2023
    [arXiv] [Code]

    YOSO designed a light-weight feature pyramid aggregator and a seperable dynamic convolution attention module to speed up the segmentation pipeline. To the best of our knowledge, YOSO is the first real-time panoptic segmentation framework with competitive performance compared to state-of-the-art methods.
    Exploring Vision Transformers as Diffusion Learners
    He Cao, Jianan Wang, Tianhe Ren, Xianbiao Qi, Yihao Chen , Yuan Yao , Lei Zhang
    arXiv preprint arXiv:2212.13771
    [arXiv] [Code Coming Soon]

    We are using plain vit structure as diffusion backbone and propose IU-ViT and ASCEND which close the performance gap between ViT and U-Net and we're the first work to explore one-stage large resolution text to image generation.
    Make Sharpness-Aware Minimization Stronger: A Sparsified Perturbation Approach
    Peng Mi, Li Shen, Tianhe Ren, Yiyi Zhou, Xiaoshuai Sun , Rongrong Ji , Dacheng Tao
    Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS), 2022
    [arXiv] [Code]

    SparseSAM is an efficient variant of SAM optimizer achieved by computing a sparse perturbation based on fisher information and dynamic sparse training.
    TRAR: Routing the Attention Spans in Transformers for Visual Question Answering
    Yiyi Zhou, Tianhe Ren, Chaoyang Zhu , Xiaoshuai Sun , Jianzhuang Liu , Xinghao Ding , Mingliang Xu , Rongrong Ji
